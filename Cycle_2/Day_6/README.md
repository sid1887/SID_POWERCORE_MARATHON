# Cycle 2 – Day 6 | Minimal Entanglement Analogue Circuit (Verilog)

## Summary
To close out the Applied Logic / Quantum day, implemented a small reversible-classical Verilog circuit that mimics the structure of quantum entanglement generation (H + CNOT). While real entanglement cannot be done in Verilog, the classical analogue demonstrates bit-correlation after a conditional flip.

## Concepts Reinforced
- Quantum entanglement uses H + CNOT
- Classical reversible circuits can mimic correlation patterns
- q1_out = q1_in XOR q0_in acts as the classical CNOT
- q0_out passes through the “superposition collapse” state
